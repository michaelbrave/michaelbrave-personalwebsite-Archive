Build In The Open

May 28 2020

Why have my notes be public? This is in large part due to two different ideas.

The first is that building in the open aka “building with the garage door open” allows for better feedback loops with other people. People can tell me where/how I’m wrong, maybe my mistakes/wisdom/failing can help someone else, those who are curious about me can know what I’m thinking and instead of having something private I can instead be building a kind of resource that could create a kind of creation momentum.

Keeping my notes public is inspired by this and this .

The second idea is along the lines of “researchers who close the door get more done, those who keep it open do more important work”. Collaboration, feedback and testing are how you end up solving the right problems instead of working on something nobody cares about, because often our first attempts at solving a problem are incomplete. I believe this idea came from Karl Friston .
