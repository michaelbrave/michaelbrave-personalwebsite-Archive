If It's Worth Doing Well, It's Worth Doing Poorly

May 30 2020

I once heard a saying that if “something is worth doing well, then it’s worth doing poorly” in this particular case it was in reference to keeping a diet and was along the lines of “done is better than perfect” or “better something than nothing”. So keeping with the diet example, every time I’ve failed at keeping a diet it’s been something along the lines of quitting because “I already messed up yesterday”, while the times I’ve been able to keep a diet were the times I was allowed to make mistakes, or when I had already factored in something like a cheat day. By aiming for perfect the outcome became less likely.

I believe that blogging is something I should do, I like writing and I think my ideas are worth sharing or at least being collected somewhere. I wish I had started sooner, but like they say “the best time to plant a tree was 40 years ago, the second best time is now”. I find myself hesitating to put my notes online because I feel like they may be too rough and unpolished, or that maybe I don’t know enough to write about it. But we learn by doing, and we refine our ideas when we share them and we when make them into something instead of leaving them at just an idea. Showing things before they’re ready make them better, it makes a shorter feedback loop so there can be more iterations and earlier in the process while larger changes can still happen.

But most of all I think of this. I would rather someone wash up as best they can than never bathe at all. I would rather see some progress than to give up because it would never be enough.

If it’s worth doing, then it’s worth doing poorly. Perfection is the enemy of good and good is better than perfect.
