You can’t trust anything to be there tomorrow, people leave or die, currency loses value, governments fail, websites disappear
Every freedom we have could one day disappear, it has happened before, vigilance and care, and a long memory are required to keep it.
Living in the moment, mindfulness, expecting things to be temporary allows you to enjoy them today, ironically making them more likely to be there in the future
Buddhist idea: all things are impermanent, our suffering stems from our expectations for things to remain the same, we could instead see the changes as a chance to learn and to grow. Accept what is, as it is. Suffering is caused by the thought, not the thing itself
The impermanence of things allows us the chance to continually learn from them. This is how a marriage of 50 years could still be learning and getting to know each other, because they do not stay the same. There is beauty in that idea.
