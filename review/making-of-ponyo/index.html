The Making of Ponyo - Documentary

May 28 2020

Part 1 - Ponyo is here#

Yesterday I was fortunate to find PBS - The Making of Ponyo and it was insightful into the of someone I’ve alway looked up to profesionally, Hayao Miyazaki.

I think the best parts weren’t the parts they focused on, they were mostly trying to capture his process (which if I’m being honest is very similar to video game concept art processes), but what was even better was watching him procrastinate, and search for inspiration and dealing with his own internal doubts. It made him a lot more human to see him struggle with some of the same feelings and obstacles that I sometimes struggle with, and it was cool to see him put something together despite all of that, and to watch him trying to hone his craft and push his own limits of not just his technical ability to make art but also to push his creativity further than before.

It also showed some of his other weaknesses like being grumpy or a less than stellar dad. These made him more relateable too, though I imagine he wishes those points would have been cut more or glossed over more. It’s a reminder to myself to build in the open, to be vulnerable and to be true.
